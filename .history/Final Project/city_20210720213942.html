Welcome to Nome!

Population: 3,850 Residents
First established in 1901 the city of Nome has been home for profit seeking gold diggers to locals who subsist off of the land, gathering, fishing, and hunting from the local plants and fauna.

What Nome is most likely most known for is the yearly Iditarod race, which consists of teams of dog mushers traveling the 938 mile journey from Anchorage to Nome. The origins of this race came about in 1925 when an outbreak of Diptheria was ravaging the city with no medecine to treat it. Due to blizzards grounding airplanes, not that they would be able to make the jouney to begin with, dog mushing was the only way to make the journey, Dog sled teams as a relay it took 5 days and temperatures with wind chill went as low at -85 degrees fahrenheit.
